About Me

I am a computational astrophysicist and Forrest Fellow at the International Centre for Radio Astronomy Research (ICRAR) at the University of Western Australia. I received my PhD at the University of Victoria in Canada. Before joining ICRAR, I was a Kavli Fellow at the Institute for the Physics and Mathematics of the Universe (IPMU) at the University of Tokyo.

My research focuses constraining fundamental physics by applying AI to large volumes of galaxy data from top telescopes and supercomputer simulations. I am particularly interested in simulation-based inference and computer vision as tools for revealing how galaxies form and the nature of dark matter.
